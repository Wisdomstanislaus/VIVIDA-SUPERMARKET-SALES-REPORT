This report provides a structured pre-analysis of VIVIDA Supermarket’s sales performance from January to March 2019. The dashboard shows revenue, income, quantity sold, product performance, city contribution, hourly trends, and branch-level income to assess operational efficiency and revenue drivers.
Project Overview
This report provides a structured pre-analysis of VIVIDA Supermarket’s sales performance from January to March 2019. The dashboard consolidates revenue, income, quantity sold, product performance, city contribution, payment behavior, hourly trends, and branch-level income to assess operational efficiency and revenue drivers.
Aim
To evaluate sales performance, profitability distribution, customer purchasing behavior, and operational concentration risks for Q1 2019.
Objectives
Assess total revenue, income, and sales volume performance.
Identify highest-performing cities, products, branches, and hours.
Evaluate payment method contribution.
Detect temporal trends (monthly and hourly).
Identify performance concentration and dependency risks.
Translate findings into operational and strategic implications.
Hypothesis
H1: Sales performance is concentrated in specific cities and product categories.
H2: Revenue leadership does not necessarily align with income (profit) leadership.
H3: Payment method preference significantly influences revenue flow.
H4: Sales decline across months indicates potential demand instability.

Key Insights
1. Sequential Revenue Decline (Structural Concern)
Sales declined from $110,754.16 (Jan) to $92,589.88 (Feb) — a ~16.4% drop within one month.
March recovered partially but did not surpass January.
This suggests either:
Post-holiday demand normalization
Weak retention
Promotional decay
Without intervention, this trajectory risks compounding quarterly weakness.
2. Revenue vs Income Compression
Total sales: $307,587.38
Total income: $15,379.37
~5% margin indicates thin profitability. Any operational inefficiency could erase profit.
Revenue scale exists. Margin strength does not.
3. City Revenue Distribution is Nearly Even (Low Geographic Concentration Risk)
Naypyitaw: $105,303.53
Yangon: $101,143.21
Mandalay: $101,140.64
Difference between highest and lowest is only ~$4,163.
Revenue stability across cities reduces regional dependency risk.
4. Cash Still Dominates, But E-Wallet is Competitive
Cash: $106,863.40
E-wallet: $104,755.34
Digital payment adoption is nearly equal to cash — this indicates transition potential.
Credit card lags at $95,968.64.
Opportunity: incentivize digital for data capture & cost optimization.
5. Volume Leadership ≠ Income Leadership
Electronic accessories lead quantity (971 units),
but Food & beverages lead income ($2,673.56).
This implies:
Higher margin per unit in Food & beverages
Electronics may be high-volume but lower margin
Volume without margin is vanity.
6. Evening Revenue Spike
7 PM: $37,809.06 — highest hourly sales.
Customer traffic concentrates late evening.
Operational staffing must align.
7. Branch Profit Leadership
Chin leads income at $2,749.51.
Rakhine lowest at $2,387.14.
Spread (~$362 difference) suggests moderate performance variance but no extreme underperformance.
Operational / Business Implications
Margin vulnerability requires cost discipline.
Evening staffing and inventory replenishment critical.
January peak suggests event-driven sales.
Balanced city performance allows regional replication strategies.
Digital payment adoption opportunity for loyalty data integration.
Challenges & Risks Identified
Declining monthly revenue trajectory.
Low net income margin (~5%).
High dependency on peak hour concentration.
Potential under-optimization of high-rated products.
Limited visible growth momentum.
Strategic Recommendations
Implement Q2 retention campaign to counter monthly decline.
Analyze cost structure to improve margin beyond 5%.
Expand Food & beverages promotion (highest income driver).
Incentivize E-wallet usage to surpass cash.
Introduce bundled offers during non-peak hours.
Optimize inventory for evening demand spike.
Benchmark Rakhine branch operational practices.
Expected Outcomes
Margin improvement to 7–8%.
Stabilized monthly revenue growth.
Improved digital payment penetration.
Higher per-unit profitability.
Reduced hour-based revenue volatility.
Next Steps
Conduct margin breakdown per product category.
Perform cohort analysis on January customers.
Review promotional calendar impact.
Audit branch-level operational efficiency.
Model forecast for Q2 based on trend line.
Conclusion
VIVIDA Supermarket demonstrates solid revenue scale and geographic balance but faces structural risks in declining monthly sales and thin margins. Performance leadership is distributed across cities and products, indicating operational consistency. However, profitability optimization, demand stabilization, and digital payment leverage are essential to transition from revenue sustainability to profit strength.
